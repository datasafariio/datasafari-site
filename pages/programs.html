Education ladder

Three pathways. One mission.

From first encounter with data to deploying real AI — our three programs form a progressive ladder for every learner in Tanzania.

Ages 14–18 · Secondary Schools

Data Wizards

Data Wizards is our flagship secondary school programme. Students go from zero to data-fluent in 12 weeks — learning to collect, clean, analyse, and present real data from their own communities.

  • Module 1 — What is Data?Types of data, sources in everyday life, why data matters for Tanzania.
  • Module 2 — Data CollectionSurvey design, observation protocols, ethical data gathering.
  • Module 3 — Spreadsheet FundamentalsGoogle Sheets / Excel — cleaning, sorting, filtering, pivot tables.
  • Module 4 — VisualisationCharts, graphs, storytelling with data for real audiences.
  • Module 5 — Introduction to StatisticsMean, median, mode, correlation — no advanced maths required.
  • Module 6 — Community Data ProjectTeams investigate a local problem using real data, present findings.

University Level · Club Format

AI Wizards

AI Wizards brings no-code and low-code AI tools to university students through a vibrant club format. Using Orange Data Mining and Google Teachable Machine, students build and deploy real models without deep programming experience.

  • Track 1 — AI LiteracyWhat is AI, ML, and deep learning? Use cases in Africa and globally.
  • Track 2 — Orange Data MiningVisual data workflows, classification, clustering — no code required.
  • Track 3 — Teachable MachineTrain image and sound classifiers using Google's Teachable Machine platform.
  • Track 4 — Ethics & BiasFairness in AI, bias in training data, responsible deployment in Tanzania.
  • Capstone — Club ProjectsTeams build and present AI solutions addressing real university or community problems.

3-Day Intensive · Open Enrolment

AI Bootcamp

The AI Bootcamp is Data Safari's most intensive offering — a three-day sprint that takes participants from raw data to a deployed AI model. Open to university graduates, professionals, and advanced secondary students.

  • Day 1 — FoundationsPython essentials, Pandas, data wrangling, exploratory analysis, and feature engineering on a real Tanzania dataset.
  • Day 2 — BuildScikit-learn, model selection, training, evaluation, cross-validation, and hyperparameter tuning.
  • Day 3 — DeployStreamlit / Gradio deployment, model cards, ethics documentation, and live demo presentations to judges.
